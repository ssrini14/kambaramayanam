## Terminology correction

The opening section of Kamba Ramayanam is titled **பாயிரம் (Pāyiram)** — "prologue/preface" — **not** "Kadavul Vazhthu." No separately-titled "Kadavul Vazhthu" unit was found in the source; some commentators may apply that functional label to the first Pāyiram verses, but the section name is Pāyiram. *(Verify whether any printed edition labels a sub-group this way.)*

Structure: 6 Kāṇḍams, ~113 padalams (Wikipedia) / 123 Wikisource entries, ~10,569 verses.

## Recommended flagship verse — Pāyiram verse 12

**Confidence: HIGH for text, judgment call for "flagship" suitability.**
**Source:** https://ta.wikisource.org/wiki/கம்பராமாயணம்/பாயிரம் (verse 12 of 12)
**Edition caveat:** Tamil Wikisource numbering; may differ in printed editions.

Tamil (verify against print — minor Unicode artifacts possible in the fetched text):
```
நாடிய பொருள் கைகூடும் ஞானமும் புகழும் உண்டாம்
வீடியல் வழி அது ஆக்கும் வேரி அம் கமலை நோக்கும்
நீடிய அரக்கர் சேனை நீறு பட்டு அழிய வாகை
சூடிய சிலை இராமன் தோள் வலி கூறுவார்க்கே
```

English (paraphrase): "For those who sing of the arm-strength of Rama of the bow — who wore
the garland of victory as the vast demon armies were reduced to ash — all desired objects
will be attained; wisdom and fame will arise; his bow-path becomes the way to liberation;
and the fragrant lotus-dweller [Lakshmi] will look upon them."

**Why it fits a landing page:** It is the phala-śruti (benedictory close) of the prologue —
the "why read this" verse. Names Rama, names his defining act (destroying the demon armies),
and promises the four puruṣārthas + liberation + Lakshmi's grace to those who engage his
story. Self-contained and complete; functions as an invitation to the reader.

## Best "avatāra-purpose" verse (the dharma theme) — Tiru Avatāra Paḍalam, v. 17

**Location:** Bāla Kāṇḍam, Tiru Avatāra Paḍalam (திரு அவதாரப் படலம்), 5th padalam, verse 17.
**Confidence: HIGH for text; MEDIUM as "flagship" (it is embedded in a narrative scene).**
**Source:** https://ta.wikisource.org/wiki/கம்பராமாயணம்/பால_காண்டம்/திரு_அவதாரப்_படலம்

Tamil (verify against print):
```
என்றனர், இடர் உழந்து, இறைஞ்சி ஏத்தலும்,
மன்றல் அம் துளவினான், 'வருந்தல்; வஞ்சகர்-
தம் தலை அறுத்து, இடர் தணிப்பென் தாரணிக்கு;
ஒன்று நீர் கேண்ம்' என, உரைத்தல் மேயினான்:
```

English (paraphrase): When the suffering gods bowed and praised him, the tulasi-wearing Lord
said: "Grieve not; I will cut off the heads of the deceivers and relieve the earth of its
distress. Hear me in one thing." — Vishnu's explicit declaration of avatāra purpose.

This is the closest Kamban parallel to **Bhagavad Gita 4.8** (`paritrāṇāya sādhūnām, vināśāya
ca duṣkṛtām, dharma-saṃsthāpanārthāya sambhavāmi yuge yuge`). Ideal to pair on an
"avatāra purpose" panel.

## Cross-references (for the verse-linking model)

- **Gita 4.7–4.8** (verified, holy-bhagavad-gita.org): yadā yadā hi dharmasya... / paritrāṇāya
  sādhūnām... — the decline-of-dharma → divine descent theme. Direct narrative parallel:
  Tiru Avatāra Paḍalam vv. 8–19 (gods lament Ravana's adharma; Vishnu declares descent).
- **Raghuveera Gadyam** (Vedanta Desika, 96 gadyam verses praising Rama's valor): Pāyiram
  v.12's "தோள் வலி கூறுவார்க்கே" (those who sing of Rama's arm-strength) mirrors the Gadyam's
  entire purpose. *Specific phrase-to-verse parallels need the Gadyam text — not yet fetched.*
- **Nammāḻvār's Tiruvāymoḻi** (1,102 verses): tradition-level connection (Rama as Vishnu
  avatāra; devotion to his feet) is strong; *specific Rama decads need specialist sources
  (A.K. Ramanujan translations, Śrīvaiṣṇava Eedu commentary) — not yet confirmed.*
